Research Interests

I am a researcher in theoretical and computational astrophysics, with a focus on magnetohydrodynamics (MHD), plasma physics, and turbulence. My work centers on dynamo processes that drive the amplification and maintenance of magnetic fields in astrophysical plasmas. I study both fundamental mechanisms and astrophysical applications of dynamos through a combination of theoretical analysis and high-resolution numerical simulations. These efforts span a broad range of environments, including stellar convection zones, planetary atmospheres, and accretion disks around black holes. In recent years, I have become particularly interested in developing effective dynamo models within general relativistic MHD (GRMHD) simulations to enable self-consistent co-evolution of accretion flows and magnetic field generation. I also work on topics more broadly related to turbulence. This includes studies of primordial magnetic fields potentially generated by turbulent processes in the early universe.
